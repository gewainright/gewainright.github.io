how it works

Deploy. Ingest.
Return a state.

Three steps. The intelligence stays at the asset. What comes back upstream is a state, not a firehose.

01

Mount the unit

An OSREP edge processing unit mounts at each monitoring point on your equipment. It connects to whatever sensors are already wired there — or new ones where needed. No rip-and-replace. The unit handles multi-sensor ingestion natively: acoustic, vibration, thermal, electrical, process.

02

Learn on-unit

After a short commissioning window, the unit has built a model of this specific asset at this specific point. All learning happens on the hardware — no data leaves, no cloud loop, no per-device tuning from your team. Identical units produce different models on different machines.

03

Emit a state

From that point on, the unit continuously monitors all its sensor inputs and emits a single state: nominal, degrading, or fault — with confidence and time-in-state. That state is what travels upstream. Your operators get signal, not noise.

Why state and not data? Raw sensor streams are expensive to transmit, store, and interpret at scale. A state is an already-interpreted answer. When it changes, you know something changed — and the unit can tell you which input drove it. You get the intelligence without the data tax.

The unit is sensor-agnostic. Our core work is in acoustic and vibration signals — but the on-unit architecture will ingest any signal with a stationary baseline. You bring the sensors already on your equipment; we bring the intelligence layer on top of them.

acoustic

Ultrasound

Failure signatures that appear in the ultrasonic band — bearing friction, cavitation, turbulence, valve chatter, electrical arcing. High-frequency acoustic is often the earliest indicator of mechanical degradation.

vibration

Accelerometer

Mechanical signatures of rotating and reciprocating equipment — imbalance, misalignment, bearing race defects, gear-mesh faults, looseness, resonance. The unit learns the harmonic fingerprint of each specific machine.

radial axial triaxial
thermal

Temperature

Bearing hot-spots, motor winding rise, heat exchanger fouling, coolant anomalies. Thermal inputs contextualize acoustic and vibration readings — a bearing fault reads differently at 40°C vs 110°C.

thermocouple RTD IR
process

Pressure & flow

Line pressure, differential pressure, flow rate. Essential for pump and compressor health — cavitation, blockage, and valve wear all present in process data before they escalate to mechanical failure.

4–20 mA HART
electrical

Current & power

Motor current signature analysis, power factor, voltage imbalance. Electrical signals carry the mechanical load signature — rotor bar faults, coupling issues, and bearing wear all leave electrical traces.

what we return

A state, not a stream.

Raw sensor data stays on the unit. What travels upstream is already interpreted — actionable without any further processing on your side.

◉

Continuous machine state

Every unit emits a rolling state: nominal, degrading, or fault. State comes with a confidence value and time-in-state. It updates in real time. If it hasn't changed, you don't need to do anything.

⌥

Input attribution

When state changes, the unit tells you which sensor input drove it and by how much. You get the cause, not just the symptom — bearing acoustic up 40%, temperature nominal, vibration nominal. That's a bearing starting to go.

⟁

Fleet-level visibility

Deploy units across multiple points on a single asset, or across an entire fleet. Each unit reports its own state. Your operations layer sees a clean grid of states — not a wall of waveforms nobody has time to read.

Where do the states go? We output over standard industrial protocols — Modbus, OPC-UA, MQTT — so states drop into your existing SCADA, historian, or operations platform without a new integration stack. We can also provide a lightweight web dashboard if you need one.
